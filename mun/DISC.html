Topic Area Summaries

The question of Internet Espionage and Cybersecurity

As the world moves into the age of the internet, the damage of internet espionage can cause and the corresponding need for importance of cybersecurity is only increasing. The rapid development of technology has increasingly rendered the existing, traditional legal systems unwieldy and obsolete. For this reason, internet espionage runs amok in today's world, from China's reported acts of cyber espionage against the USA to Israel and the United States' alleged use of the Stuxnet virus against Iran. A several questions must be raised in the solving of this issue. How much evidence should be needed to convict an entity of cyber espionage? What will be the sanctions against such entities? What measures can be placed against these very advancing acts of espionage?

The question of Illicit Small Arms

Small arms refers to weapons like revolvers and self-loading pistols, rifles and carbines, assault rifles, submachine guns and light machine guns. Because of the inherent easy to carry and conceal nature of these weapons, the trade of illicit small arms is proving to be a challenge, especially in chaotic conflict areas, where the problem is most prominent in. The harms these weapons inflict are great – thousands are killed every year due to the influx of such weapons into the mentioned conflict zones. An effective solution to problem not only account the extreme logistical difficulties inherent in this issue, but also take into factors like national sovereignty that may arise in tackling the problem.

The question of Private Military Companies

PMCs (Private Military Companies) are subject to much controversy. In 2001, the United Nations General Assembly passed the United Nations Mercenary Convention, which prohibited the recruitment, training, use, and financing of mercenaries. Yet, it is clear that a more comprehensive solution is needed. Since the passing of the convention, nations like USA, who are not signatories to the convention, have used PMCs like Blackwater, now known as Academi, that have become embroiled in many human rights violation accusations. With this issue rapidly growing and the United Nations Mercenary Convention less relevant, it is clear that new answers regarding the legitimacy PMCs, the rules they should operate by, and the monitoring of these organizations is needed.
